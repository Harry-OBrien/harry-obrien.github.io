Experience

Hyperloop Manchester

Lead Software Developer and Head of Electronics

As Head software and hardware developer for the University of Manchester Hyperloop program, I was tasked to design and lead the development of the entire digital system of the pod. Projects

Mint Crispr

Mint Crispr is composed of a mobile app, written using flutter, and a custom embedded electronic device used to rapidly diagnose disease in developing countries. As part of a research project into low-cost methods for diagnosis, my work focuses on a system for a doctor to take a small blood sample from a patient and have a result with minutes on their phone and the cloud.

iMech-E

iMechE is a project in which an autonomous aircraft must complete several challenges. My task was for the aircraft to navigate the course and detect twelve 2x2m squares and identify a letter written on them. The image shown is part of a set of 10,000 images of airports programatically captured from Google Maps and modified to train and test a computer vision AI. This project is in collaboration with BAE and the University of Manchester UAV Society and combines aeronautical, mechanical and software Engineering from the entire team.

BAE Drone Swarm

I have build a Drone capable of tracking other drones mid-flight using the Y.O.L.O algorithm and stereo cameras for depth perception and better environmental awareness. We are working on code to have multiple drones in the air capable of tracking then taking down the opponent's aircraft. This project is in collaboration with BAE and the University of Manchester UAV Society.

Raspberry Pi Bare-Bones Operating System

I am in the process of developing my own bare-bones operating system for use on the raspberry Pi. I have implemented communication over UART and in the process of developing a basic HDMI output based off of the Linux video drivers.

Google Hash Code is an event that takes place online in which students complete 1 problem with 5 different input files. Although my team did not qualify for the final, we were able to achieve full points in the example round and have gained valuable experience to compete again this year coming.

I participated in the first-ever Graphene Hackathon at the Graphene Engineering Innovation Center as a team of six. We worked extremely well as a team using graphene ink and C++ on an Arduino.
